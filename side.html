Walk along the footsteps of history

Side is an ancient Greek city belonging to historical Pamphylia which is currently located on the southern Mediterranean coast of Turkey. Although Side is a small city, it is worth a visit and if you love history you wonâ€™t be disappointed in this city.

You can start by taking a leisurely stroll along the scenic narrow streets, which are full of gift shops, especially jewelry and gold shops. In the shops you will see a colorful display of different kinds of spices, dried fruits, fruit teas, mainly apple and pomegranate tea. As local souvenirs you can buy Turkish handicraft such as ceramics, tiles, mosaic glasses, carpets and kilims, stone jewellery and lampshades. Remember that you it is a common and accepted practice to haggle in shops for prices. On the side-streets you can find more peace and quiet and some pretty local houses and gardens. If you feel thirsty or hot, several stands along the market street sell freshly-squeezed orange juice and delicious turkish ice-cream.

If you are a fan of antiquity, Side has lots of stunning Roman and Greek-era monuments to see, including the well-preserved city walls, which lead to an entrance to the historical sites through the Hellenistic main gate. Along the way, you will find ruins of an amphitheatre, a public bath, an aqueduct, a library, a monumental fountain (nymphaeum), the market place (State Agora) and of course several temples. The fountains are on a small road leading out from the temple and amphitheatre, about five minutes from the centre and on the walk to the local bus station.

The majestic ancient temples of Apollo and Athena are located at the end of Side's peninsula. A short walk through the main market street, turning left at the bottom of the street by the harbour, you reach these temples. The view of the white marble columns against the deep blue sea and clear blue skyline is quite striking and provides a perfect spot for taking breathtaking photos. Another important temple, is the temple of Men dedicated to the god of moon. We recommend visiting the temples in late evening in order to enjoy the beautiful sunset.

There is no entrance fee for the visiting the amphitheatre and the temples, but If you interested in soaking up even more history, you can also visit the small Side museum which costs about 10 lira (3 euros) for entry. Inside you can find old Roman baths, Byzantine burials and a collection of Roman statues and artefacts. Outside there are gardens with monuments and views over the harbour. Also being inside the museum can be both educational and a good way to spend some time out of the sun. If you are travelling in summer, it is better to visit the historical ruins in the morning or evening to avoid the heat. It can be dry in the middle of the day and difficult to find any shades. Also, since the path through the ruins is rather long, we recommend wearing comfortable walking shoes, hat and suncream.

The beach resort city of Alanya 60 kms away is another nearby location that you can visit if you are staying longer in Side. It is known for its distinctive castle, a Seljuk era citadel, harbour, caves, long strand of sandy beach and abundant water sports.
